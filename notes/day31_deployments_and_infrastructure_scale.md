## Key Takeaways

- Successfully completed the role-play focused on Infrastructure as Code, CloudFormation, CDK, and Systems Manager. Explanations were delivered in clear, business-friendly language with emphasis on consistency, risk reduction, and operational efficiency.

- **Infrastructure as Code** allows infrastructure to be defined and managed through code rather than manual processes. This improves consistency, repeatability, and reduces human error across environments.

- **AWS CloudFormation** enables the creation and management of AWS resources through templates. It supports automation, version control, and the ability to reliably recreate environments.

- **AWS CDK** provides a more developer-friendly way to define infrastructure using familiar programming languages, which are then converted into CloudFormation templates under the hood.

- **AWS Systems Manager** offers centralized visibility and control over EC2 instances and other resources. It helps automate routine operational tasks such as patching, configuration management, and running commands at scale.

- The overall value of these tools lies in reducing manual effort, improving consistency, lowering operational risk, and allowing teams to focus more on business outcomes rather than infrastructure maintenance.
